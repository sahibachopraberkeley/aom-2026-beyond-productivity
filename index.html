About the workshop

Designing research on how AI is reshaping work.

This Professional Development Workshop is designed to help scholars build stronger research on how AI is reshaping work: how it is adopted, how it changes evaluation and feedback, and how it reshapes skill. It takes place Friday, July 31, 2026, 8:00–10:00 AM ET at the Loews Hotel (Congress C).

The session opens with five short framework presentations, each introducing a research approach from the leader's own work. Participants then join a leader in facilitated roundtables to develop these approaches into their own projects: sharpening research questions, pairing data with methods, and mapping concrete next steps toward a viable study.

To help us balance the roundtables, we recommend completing the registration form by Friday, July 24, 2026.

The presentations

Five frameworks for studying AI in organizations.

Each leader introduces a research framework developed in their own work: how questions are framed, mechanisms articulated, and designs selected. Topics span three themes: measurement and early-stage theory building, evaluation under AI, and skill formation and adaptation. Rank the topics on the registration form to help us assign roundtable groups.

R1
Theme 1 · Measurement & early-stage theory

Generating & evaluating candidate mechanisms

Systematically generating and evaluating candidate mechanisms in settings characterized by overdetermination, combining computational tools with human judgment to improve transparency and rigor in early-stage theory building.

Natalie Carlson · Wharton, University of Pennsylvania

Assistant Professor at Wharton; her recent Strategic Management Journal work sets foundational guidelines for using LLMs to annotate data in management research.

Faculty · Site

R2
Theme 2 · Evaluation under AI

How AI-mediated information reshapes evaluation

How AI-mediated information changes evaluation processes, with emphasis on isolating genuine efficiency gains from judgment distortions in selection and feedback.

Charles Ayoubi · ESSEC Business School

Assistant Professor at ESSEC; studies how organizations generate, evaluate, and diffuse innovation, including with generative AI.

Faculty · Site · Scholar

R3
Theme 3 · Skill formation & adaptation

Technology–skill complementarity, historically

Historical analysis of technology–skill complementarity and the co-evolution of tools, tasks, and training institutions.

Michael Impink · HEC Paris

Assistant Professor of Strategy at HEC Paris; studies AI startups, digital communication, and technology strategy.

Faculty · Site · Scholar

R4
Theme 1 · Measurement & early-stage theory

Measuring adoption from behavioral trace data

Designing measures from behavioral trace data that capture adoption intensity, trajectories, and socially embedded diffusion, and evaluating how measurement choices shape the theoretical claims we can make.

Mathijs de Vaan · UC Berkeley (Haas)

Associate Professor at Berkeley Haas; studies social networks, the science of science, and AI and the future of work.

Faculty · Site · Scholar

R5
Theme 2 · Evaluation under AI

Work & evaluation in collaborative settings

How AI transforms the relationship between work and evaluation in collaborative settings, including practical tools for identifying AI use in observational data.

Sahiba Chopra · UC Berkeley (Haas)

PhD candidate at Berkeley Haas and D-Lab fellow; studies how generative AI reshapes work, evaluation, and organizational design.

Site · Scholar

How the session runs

Framework presentations, then facilitated roundtables.

Each leader gives a framework presentation of about 13 minutes. The session then moves into facilitated roundtables, where participants adapt these approaches to their own research.

  1. 5 min

    Welcome & introduction

    Goals of the workshop and an overview of AI's organizational consequences.

  2. 65 min

    Five framework presentations

    Each leader presents a research framework from their own work (about 13 minutes each): how questions are framed, mechanisms articulated, and designs selected.

  3. 45 min

    Facilitated roundtables

    Self-select into a roundtable chaired by a workshop leader and adapt these approaches to your own research, the core working time of the session.

  4. 5 min

    Synthesis

    Key insights pooled across the roundtables.
