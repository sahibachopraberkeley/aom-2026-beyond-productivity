Beyond Productivity

Designing Research on AI Adoption, Evaluation, and Training

AI is not just making work faster — it is rewiring how organizations measure adoption, evaluate people, coordinate, and build skill. This is a working session to turn those shifts into research you can actually run.

📅 Fri, July 31, 2026 · 8:00–10:00 AM ET 📍 Loews Hotel · Congress C 🎯 TIM · OB · OMT
Register your interest → Meet the roundtables

Why this room, for two hours

Everyone is studying AI. The hard part is the research design.

Scholars of AI's organizational consequences keep hitting the same wall — not a shortage of questions, but a shortage of designs that hold up. This PDW is built around the four choices that quietly decide whether an early-stage project becomes a real contribution.

01

Beyond binary adoption

Measure intensity, trajectories, and socially embedded diffusion — not just "used it / didn't."

02

Mechanisms, systematically

Generate and screen candidate mechanisms in settings where everything is overdetermined.

03

Evaluation, rewritten

Theorize selection and feedback when AI changes the information they're built on.

04

Skill in motion

Study the co-evolution of AI, tasks, and the training systems that lag behind them.

You leave with sharper questions, clearer mechanisms, and better-aligned empirical strategies — plus a table of people to keep building with.

Three themes, five tables

Pick the challenge closest to your work.

After short teaser talks, you self-select into one parallel roundtable — each chaired by a leader who studies this for a living. Here's the map.

The research seeds

Five leaders. Five open puzzles.

You'll rank these on the form — bring your own data and context to one.

R1
Theme 01 · Measurement

Measuring adoption from behavioral trace data

How do we design measures that capture adoption intensity, trajectories, and socially embedded diffusion — and how do those measurement choices shape the theoretical claims we can make?

Mathijs de Vaan · UC Berkeley (Haas)

R2
Theme 01 · Measurement

Generating & evaluating candidate mechanisms

In overdetermined settings, how do we systematically generate and screen mechanisms — pairing computational tools with human judgment for transparent, rigorous early-stage theory?

Natalie Carlson · Wharton, University of Pennsylvania

R3
Theme 02 · Evaluation

How AI-mediated information reshapes evaluation

When AI sits between evaluator and evaluated, how do we isolate genuine efficiency gains from judgment distortions in selection and feedback?

Charles Ayoubi · ESSEC Business School

R4
Theme 02 · Evaluation · Organizer

Work & evaluation in collaborative settings

How does AI transform the relationship between work and evaluation when people collaborate — and what practical tools let us identify AI use in observational data?

Sahiba Chopra · UC Berkeley (Haas)

R5
Theme 03 · Skill

Technology–skill complementarity, historically

What does the history of technological change teach us about the co-evolution of tools, tasks, and training institutions — and where AI will bind next?

Michael Impink · HEC Paris

    Insights pooled across tables; takeaways shared with participants afterward.
